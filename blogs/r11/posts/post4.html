8/4/16 Weekly Recap

This article is about what Room 11 has been doing from 4/4/16 - 8/4/16

Posted by Moss Pelvin and George Hyink on April 8, 2016
Ugh, what a week! It has been slow, repetitive and boring, with almost no variety. Please be kind to our writers because they have had to deal to all of the incredibly hard work. They should be paid for this. (Hint, hint Mr K)

Inquiry

For inquiry we finished our research, and had a chat about presentation. Mr K recommends a 2 page essay, for all you struggling peoples out there.

Literacy

For Literacy we have been writing poetry and studying segues. Just to show dedication (not), one of our writers, Moss Pelvin, has memorized the Robert Frost poem, Ice and Fire. So here it is:

“Some say the world will end in fire, Some say in ice. From what I’ve tasted of desire I hold with those who favor fire. But if I had to perish twice, I think I know enough of hate. To say that for destruction, ice Is also great And would suffice.”

Also, here is an example of poetry by the other writer, George Hyink.

Dawn shines upon a world of darkness
A golden world it creates
The crisp air gleams
The ground returns
Light to the cold air’s berth
Water drips over a blade of ice
A cold fire burns inside
Dawn light glows
Warm and bright
‘Til the beautiful structure no more
Sheets of white laced upon earth
All life subdued by it’s chilling breath
But cool light shines
So chill shall fade
And life take it’s world back

P.E.

In the rare PE sessions this week we played netball tag and dodgeball. Netball tag was to practice small ball skills, but the miskept score created a test for our debating skills as well. Thank Bridie for this hilarious PE mishap. As for Dodgeball, the skilled team (led by George Hyink) won three rounds to nil.

Conclusion

As you can see the week was very simple, but quite productive. Well, for our writers anyway. We hope you have been amused by our sarcasm and lightning sharp wit. See you next time.
